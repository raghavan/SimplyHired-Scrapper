Clinical Lab Scientist Data Analyst (Monogram Biosciences)

Job Code: 15335
Location: South San Francisco, CA
Category: Laboratory Jobs

Job Description:

BS/BA in chemical, physical, biological or clinical laboratory and laboratory training/experience in specialties related to molecular biology, virology, and /or tissue culture
Requires current State of California Clinical Laboratory Scientist license
Strong verbal and written communications skills are necessary
Good understanding of statistical methods and application to the analysis of complex assays
Ability to work in a collaborative, flexible environment
Strong computer skills and attention to detail are required

Job Description:

Position Summary:
Performs highly complex diagnostic procedures on clinical specimens. Compiles and evaluates data generated from testing processes. Ensures all data meets acceptable performance criteria. Authorizes and generates reports.
NOTE: Clinical Laboratory Scientists that have 7 or more years of technical experience may act as Lead Techs in the absence of a Supervisor and will have the responsibility to make technical decisions as required.
Position Description:
Supervises unlicensed laboratory staff and clinical laboratory scientists in training
Coordinates with other clinical laboratory scientists, including coordinators for safety and quality control
Follows Standard Operating Procedures for specimen processing, performing test procedures, data analysis and reporting test results
Performs appropriate QA/QC procedures
Operates and maintains equipment and instruments according to manufacturer and Monogram Clinical Reference Lab (MCRL) established procedures including:
Analysis of clinical specimens
Calibration
Preventive maintenance
Troubleshooting
May be appointed to oversee specialized processes or appointed to a unique coordinating role, such as safety or QC
Actively participates in continuing education and in service training programs for licensure
Participates in drafting and developing Standard Operating Procedures
Reagent Preparation
May participate in development of new assays and technology transfer from research in to development
May participate in vendor/product evaluation
Reviews and Reports Data from phenotypic and genotypic data analysis systems
Understands Clinical Laboratory Technician role and is able to provide technical guidance
Working Conditions:
Prolonged sitting; prolonged exposure to computer monitors
Minimal exposure to specimens and material considered infectious and/or bio-hazardous
Limited lifting/bending, walking/time on feet
Operation of electrical equipment/instruments containing heating and freezing components, moving parts, and high speed centrifugation
Use and wearing of protective garments and equipment when in the laboratory
Work schedule may be modified to meet operational requirements
Physical Demands:
Speech clarity, utilization of fingers and hands, standing-remaining on one's feet in an upright position, sitting-remaining in the normal seated position, handling-seizing, holding, grasping
Moderate physical effort (up to 30 pounds); must comply with applicable safety procedures.
Additional job duty (as assigned): (Hazardous Chemical Waste Management) Proper identification of known hazardous material in the work place.
Knowledge of personal protection measures; avoidance of accidents; knowledge of hazardous waste characteristics, hazardous waste volume generation limits, accumulation limits, spill control measures, record keeping, proper handling and container management, labeling, container and storage inspection.
Vision Requirements:
Far acuity-ability to see clearly at 20 feet or more; near acuity-ability to see clearly at 20 inches or less
Depth perception-ability to judge distance and space relationships
Color vision-ability to distinguish and identify different colors apply
